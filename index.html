Walkable Suburban Areas

Using the Grid System to accommodate tram lines and single family households

By Cameron Houde

Why a walkable suburb?

Suburban areas are heavily popular due to their cheapness and ability to accommodate large families. However, suburban areas often require a car, and almost all errands can not be accomplished by foot.

How I'm approaching walkability

Many suburban areas in North America are already capable of being walkable areas. Upgrades that can make this happen are:

  • Adding Bus Routes or Tram Lines
  • Making businesses and amenities within closer distances
  • Maintaining a good ratio of commercial buildings and residential buildings in a given area
  • Using the Grid System
